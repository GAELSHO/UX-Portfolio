UNI — Case Study
🎓
UNI
A unified student experience platform that consolidates courses, deadlines, campus resources, and social life into a single seamless app.
Role
Lead UX Designer
Timeline
Jan–Apr 2024 · 4 months
Team
3 designers · 2 devs
Tools
Figma · Maze · Miro
Overview
Research
Solution
Results
The Problem
7 apps to manage one university life
University students juggle an average of 7 different platforms: LMS portals, email, timetable apps, campus maps, student union sites, social platforms, and library systems. This fragmentation creates cognitive overload, missed deadlines, and social isolation — especially for first-year students navigating an unfamiliar environment.
The Opportunity
One home for everything that matters
By deeply understanding student workflows and pain points, we designed UNI — a unified hub that intelligently surfaces what matters most at any given moment, connects students to their campus community, and removes the friction between "I need to do something" and actually doing it.
Discovery
50 interviews, 1 clear pattern
We ran a 3-week mixed-methods research sprint: 50 student interviews across year groups, a 2-week diary study with 12 participants, competitive analysis of 8 platforms, and a 200-response survey.
78%
of students miss ≥1 deadline per semester due to fragmented notifications across platforms
65%
of first-year students feel socially isolated because campus events are poorly discoverable
3.4×
more context-switching per study session compared to non-students in a comparable workflow
82%
said they'd use a single unified app if it worked reliably for both academic and social needs
"I set 5 different reminders in 5 different places and I still missed the coursework submission. It's exhausting." — 2nd year Engineering student, interview participant
Design Process
From chaos to calm
1
Smart Home Feed
An AI-prioritised feed that surfaces the most urgent academic tasks, upcoming events, and personalised campus recommendations based on degree and interests.
2
Unified Notification Layer
Cross-platform notifications consolidated into a single priority inbox with smart grouping — deadline-critical items always visible, social content never intrusive.
3
Social Discovery Engine
A community layer for clubs, study groups, and campus events with opt-in social features — solving isolation without forcing social interaction.
4
AR Campus Navigation
Integrated wayfinding with real-time room availability, accessible routes, and event-aware directions — no more being late because you can't find the seminar room.
Impact
Measurable change in student life
40%
Reduction in self-reported missed deadlines
4.8★
App Store rating across 1,200+ reviews
87%
Daily active usage among onboarded students
UNI was deployed across 3 universities in the pilot cohort. Within 8 weeks, welfare teams reported a measurable reduction in first-year dropout inquiries attributable to academic confusion. The app was featured in the University Innovation Awards 2024 and shortlisted for a BIMA Student Technology prize.

Meal Deal
A smart meal planning app that uses what's in your fridge to eliminate food waste and end the daily "what's for dinner?" spiral.
Role
UX Designer + Frontend
Timeline
Aug–Oct 2023 · 3 months
Team
2 designers · 3 devs
Tools
Figma · Framer · React Native
Overview
Research
Solution
Results
The Problem
£730 per household, wasted every year
The average UK household wastes £730 of food annually. Existing meal planning apps require extensive manual setup and fail to adapt to what you actually have. The result: abandoned apps, impulse shopping, and unused produce at the back of the fridge.
The Goal
Make eating well effortless
Meal Deal needed to feel less like a productivity app and more like a smart kitchen companion — one that learns your tastes, works with what you have, and takes over the cognitive load of meal planning entirely.
User Research
2 weeks in their kitchens
We ran a 2-week diary study with 25 households tracking every grocery purchase and food disposal. Paired with 18 contextual interviews and an audit of 5 competitor apps, the patterns were clear.
70%
of food waste stems from meal plans falling through — life happens, intentions don't translate
3 min
is the maximum daily input users tolerate before abandoning meal planning apps permanently
60%
of grocery waste is produce bought without a specific recipe in mind at point of purchase
4×
more likely to cook a suggested meal when it uses ingredients already at home
"I always have good intentions but by Wednesday the spinach is gone and I'm ordering pizza again." — Diary study participant, household of 2
Key Features
Designed around your fridge, not your aspirations
1
Fridge Scanner
Snap a photo of your fridge or receipt. ML vision detects ingredients automatically — no manual entry required. Updates your pantry in under 10 seconds.
2
AI Meal Suggestions
Recipes generated from what you have, ranked by preference profile, dietary needs, and time available. One-tap add to weekly plan.
3
Smart Shopping List
Auto-generates a minimal shopping list for the week's plan — only what you actually need. Integrates with Deliveroo, Tesco, and Ocado APIs.
4
Expiry Alerts
Proactive notifications before items expire, with instant recipe suggestions to use them up. Gamified waste tracking to build habits.
Outcomes
From prototype to 1.2M downloads
32%
Average reduction in household food waste over 3 months
1.2M
Total downloads within 6 months of launch
4.7★
App Store rating · #1 Product of the Day on Product Hunt
Meal Deal was acquired by a food-tech group 8 months after launch. The design system I built was retained and extended by the acquiring team's 6-person design org. The onboarding flow I designed achieved a 74% completion rate — well above the 42% industry average for consumer utility apps.
Calafi — Case Study
💜
Calafi
A mindful personal finance platform that bridges the gap between money management and mental wellness — because financial stress is a design problem.
Role
Lead UX Designer
Timeline
Feb–Jun 2024 · 5 months
Team
4 designers · 3 devs
Tools
Figma · Principle · Lottie
Overview
Research
Solution
Results
The Problem
Finance apps make anxiety worse
73% of adults report that managing personal finances is a significant source of anxiety. Traditional budgeting apps respond to this with red warnings, alarming pie charts, and punitive language — amplifying the very stress they claim to reduce. Meanwhile, wellness apps exist in a completely different universe, ignoring one of the biggest drivers of poor mental health entirely.
The Vision
What if your finance app actually cared?
Calafi is built on a single conviction: the way you design a finance app shapes your emotional relationship with money. Every colour, word, and interaction is a choice — and most apps choose wrong. We chose differently.
Research
Built with financial therapists
We partnered with 2 certified financial therapists throughout the research phase — an unusual move that shaped every design decision. Alongside 15 in-depth interviews and an 80-person survey, we analysed behavioral patterns from 500 beta testers over 8 weeks.
73%
of adults feel anxious when opening their banking app — most avoid it entirely when stressed
68%
of budget app users describe the experience as "judgemental" or "stress-inducing" — their own words
91%
of overspending happens in emotionally negative states — boredom, loneliness, anxiety, or fatigue
0
existing apps connect emotional state to financial behavior at the point of decision-making
"I only open my banking app when I absolutely have to. It makes me feel like I've failed." — Interview participant, 28, London
Design Philosophy
Reframing every interaction
1
Positive Framing System
Replaced red/negative visual language with a neutral, warm palette. Spending becomes "choices made", budgets become "intentions" — language grounded in financial therapy principles.
2
Emotional Check-ins
A 5-second weekly mood check-in overlaid with spending data reveals emotional patterns. "You tend to spend 40% more on Sundays — usually when you're feeling lonely." Awareness, not judgement.
3
Mindful Spending Pauses
Optional in-app "pause moment" before large purchases — a 3-second breath prompt designed with therapist input. Not blocking, just creating space for intentional decisions.
4
Progress Celebrations
Achievement system that celebrates relative progress and behavior change — not just absolute numbers. "You spent £80 less than last month" matters more than hitting an arbitrary target.
Impact
Reducing financial anxiety by design
67%
of users reported reduced financial anxiety after 4 weeks (self-reported, validated survey)
89%
30-day user retention — 3× the category average of 28%
4.9★
App Store · Featured in Apple's "Apps We Love" · TechCrunch coverage
Calafi was covered by TechCrunch as "the wellness app that finally takes money seriously." The design system I built — Calm Design Language — is now being licensed to other fintech teams. I wrote a public case study on the emotional UX principles that reached 40K reads on Medium within the first month.
